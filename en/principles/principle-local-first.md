# Principle: Local First
- Statement: Personal knowledge, memory, context, and thinking traces should remain primarily under the subject’s control.
- Rationale: When a system reaches “second brain” territory, it touches private, high-value cognitive structures; sovereignty and exit must be real.
- Implications:
  - Design defaults should reduce involuntary extraction and centralization of personal cognition.
  - The subject can inspect, move, and revoke what Selform retains.
- Tensions & tradeoffs: Local control can reduce convenience and “seamlessness”; the friction is often ethically required.
- Anti-patterns: Treating private cognition as training data by default; making export/exit effectively impossible.
- Status: draft
- History / references: Originated in the early ISE framing; reinforced by later subject-formation ethics.
