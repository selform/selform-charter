# Principle: Constrained Progressivism
- Statement: Explore and iterate, but do not treat “progress” as an exemption from ethical responsibility or irreversible harm.
- Rationale: Small-step iteration is compatible with ethics only when the objective function and guardrails are explicit.
- Implications:
  - Make the optimization target explicit (e.g., holding complex questions, strengthening judgment) and avoid optimizing for speed/comfort by default.
  - Prefer reversible experiments; treat agency loss as an alert condition.
- Tensions & tradeoffs: Slower exploration and fewer “wow” moments; more deliberation and accountability.
- Anti-patterns: “We can always roll back” illusions; optimizing smoothness that silently increases dependency.
- Status: draft
- History / references: Emerged from “local optimum ≠ right direction” and risk-first framing.
