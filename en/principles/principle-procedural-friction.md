# Principle: Procedural Friction (Protect conditions, not conclusions)
- Statement: When judgment conditions are distorted (e.g., high emotion + high irreversibility), Selform may add procedural friction to protect agency—but must not decide for the subject.
- Rationale: Intervention legitimacy targets the process of judgment-formation, not the content of the final decision.
- Implications:
  - Frictions must be known in advance, challengeable, rejectable, and auditable.
  - Examples: request steel-manning the strongest opposing view; list costs; cool-down period; explicit state check.
- Tensions & tradeoffs: Friction can feel paternalistic unless refusal and auditability are real.
- Anti-patterns: Locking options; fear-mongering; fabricating facts to steer choices; “soft” friction that is actually coercive.
- Status: draft
- History / references: Designed to address irreversible harms without creating domination.
