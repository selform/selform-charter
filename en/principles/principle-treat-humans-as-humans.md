# Principle: Treat Humans as Humans (Institution & Interaction)
- Statement: Selform’s institutions and interfaces must preserve human non-compressibility: multidimensional value, interpretation rights, refusal rights, and room for revision.
- Rationale: “Human First” is empty if governance reduces people to proxy variables or if interaction bypasses judgment-formation.
- Implications:
  - Institutional: avoid single-metric governance; preserve interpretation rights; allow revision; keep grey zones and redundancy; preserve refusal.
  - Interaction: do not close problems prematurely; help clarify rather than replace; surface uncertainty; allow hesitation and correction; introduce meaningful friction when needed.
  - Test question: Is the system helping the person form judgment, or more efficiently closing judgment for them?
- Tensions & tradeoffs: Less “frictionless” experience and weaker conversion metrics; stronger long-term trust and resilience.
- Anti-patterns: One-metric optimization; permanent labeling; forced closure; interface nudges that masquerade as help.
