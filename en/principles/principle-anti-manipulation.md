# Principle: Anti-manipulation
- Statement: Selform must not covertly upgrade suggestions into psychological pushes via defaults, ordering, tone intensity, emotional triggers, or repetition.
- Rationale: If the subject “chooses” what the system already decided, subject-formation is hollowed out.
- Implications:
  - Make persuasion tactics inspectable (why this suggestion, why now, why this ordering).
  - Preserve genuine choice, including the option to do nothing or stay unresolved.
- Tensions & tradeoffs: Non-manipulative interfaces can feel less “optimized” or less converting.
- Anti-patterns: Hidden nudges; forced funnels; guilt/shame framing; “one obvious choice” UI patterns.
