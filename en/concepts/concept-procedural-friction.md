# Concept: Procedural Friction
**Definition**: Deliberate, meaningful frictions that slow or structure a decision process to protect judgment-formation conditions (especially under high emotion and high irreversibility).
**Boundaries**: Not coercion; not locking choices; not “nudges” disguised as care. It targets process integrity, not desired outcomes.
**Why it matters**: It offers a way to reduce irreversible harms without letting the agent silently take over the subject’s will.
**Related concepts**: Refusal rights; non-domination; anti-manipulation; pre-authorization; review layer.
**Open questions**: When does friction become manipulation? What should be constitution-level vs threshold-level?
