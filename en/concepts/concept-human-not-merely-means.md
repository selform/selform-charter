# Concept: Human Not Merely a Means
**Definition**: A person may participate in functional relations, but must not be fully reduced to function, metric containers, profit sources, or objects to be optimized.
**Boundaries**: This does not deny cooperation, exchange, or division of labor; it denies total reduction where value depends only on usefulness to a system.
**Why it matters**: Treating humans as “variables” invites manipulation and overfitting; treating them as subjects preserves agency, interpretation rights, and long-run trust.
**Related concepts**: Agency; “human as variable” overfitting; non-domination; anti-manipulation; constrained progressivism.
**Open questions**: What institutional rules preserve interpretation and refusal rights without freezing people into labels?
