# Concept: Agency (Subject Position)
**Definition**: The capacity of a person to be a source of judgment, choice, refusal, responsibility, and self-correction—rather than a managed container of outcomes.
**Boundaries**: Not “doing whatever one wants.” Agency includes reason-giving, reflection, and the right to remain unresolved; it excludes pure compliance optimized by systems.
**Why it matters**: If agency erodes, self-formation becomes hollow: problems, values, and meanings get set “for” the person rather than “by” the person-in-time.
**Related concepts**: Self-formation; refusal rights; non-domination; procedural friction; auditable reasoning interface.
**Open questions**: Which interventions protect agency vs replace it? Which rights are inalienable vs pre-authorizable?
