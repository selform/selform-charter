# Concept: “Human as Variable” (Overfitting)
**Definition**: Treating a person as a small set of proxy variables (retention, output, scores, clicks) and optimizing hard on those proxies as if they were the person.
**Boundaries**: This is more than simplification; it is structural fragility: the system learns the shadow of a person in one context, not the person as a dynamic, reflexive being.
**Why it matters**: Proxy optimization can increase short-term efficiency while eroding agency and increasing brittleness under real-world change (“black swans”). Treating humans as subjects can be slower locally yet more resilient and even antifragile over time.
**Related concepts**: Antifragility; anti-manipulation; constrained progressivism; “human not merely a means”; “don’t be evil” as refusing to sacrifice agency for computability.
**Open questions**: What institutional constraints keep systems from proxy-optimizing people? What metrics should be refused?
