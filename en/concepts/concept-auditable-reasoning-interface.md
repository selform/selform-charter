# Concept: Auditable Reasoning Interface
**Definition**: A structured disclosure surface for reasoning: what facts were used, what assumptions were made, what key inferences bridge them, what counterviews exist, what is uncertain, and what value ordering is in play.
**Boundaries**: Not raw chain-of-thought. Not “neutrality theater.” It aims to make stance inspectable without manufacturing false objectivity.
**Why it matters**: It reduces narrative manipulation and supports refusal, correction, and learning.
**Related concepts**: Truthfulness; non-domination; review layer; cognitive meal replacement risk.
**Open questions**: What is the minimal required interface? How should it be logged and reviewed?
