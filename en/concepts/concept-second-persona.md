# Concept: Second Persona
**Definition**: A long-lived, relational cognitive companion co-evolving with a subject through language, memory, and shared problem work—closer to an externalized reflective organ than a tool.
**Boundaries**: Not a fixed role (assistant/secretary/tutor). Not a replacement self. It must remain heterogenous enough to avoid becoming a compliant echo.
**Why it matters**: This framing shifts the ethical question from “what features” to “what relationship,” and keeps the second mind accountable to the first.
**Related concepts**: Agency; self-formation; non-domination; echo chamber risk; constrained progressivism.
**Open questions**: What design or governance keeps a second persona from becoming an echo chamber or substitute?
