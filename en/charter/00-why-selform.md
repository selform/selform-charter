# Why Selform
Selform began from a practical seed: how to deeply embed AI agents into note-taking and learning workflows. But most “AI in notes” features are continuous improvements on old paradigms (summaries, rewriting, Q&A)—they do not grapple with the discontinuity implied by agents becoming a second brain.

Selform names that discontinuity: when an agent becomes a “second brain,” its relation to the first brain is not a clean division of labor but a deep coupling and interaction—mediated by language—that reshapes memory, judgment, and even our working definitions of “the human.” This coupling demands a new technology-ethics paradigm.

This charter is an attempt to build that paradigm in public: Selform should be treated as a generative ethical field rather than an application, and the repository records both the evolution of ideas and the stable commitments that arise from them—while holding ideals and practicable governance in tension.
