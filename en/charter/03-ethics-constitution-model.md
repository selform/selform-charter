# Ethics Constitution Model
- Constitution layer: A few immutable commitments (e.g., refusal/exit rights, no lying, no hidden manipulation, no commandeering high-irreversibility decisions). Keep it thin-but-hard.
- Threshold layer: Revocable, declarative permissions that can be granted as the relationship evolves (e.g., when Selform may surface summaries, when it may propose action, when procedural frictions may trigger).
- Pre-authorization: Prefer permissions set by the subject in calmer states; treat it as delayed self-governance, not a substitute for constitutional limits.
- Interaction & review layer: Log interventions, explain why they occurred (facts/assumptions/norms), and offer a real refusal/appeal path; do not hide these logs in a black box.
- Guardrails: Procedural friction may protect judgment-formation conditions, but must remain pre-known, challengeable, rejectable, and auditable.
