# Ethics Constitution Model
- Constitution layer: A few immutable commitments (e.g., no substitution of the first mind, refusal rights, transparency about intervention). Freeze these in text and treat them as hard constraints.
- Threshold layer: Declarative permissions that can be granted and revoked as the relationship evolves (e.g., when Selform may surface summaries, when it may propose action).
- Interaction & review layer: Log interventions, explain why they occurred, and offer a real refusal path; do not hide these logs in a black box.
- Guardrails: Every permission includes friction that protects judgment-formation conditions without commandeering conclusions.
