# Core Risks
- Dependency: Selform must not make the first mind abdicable; it should leave space for people to hold problems themselves.
- Subjectivity erosion: Giving up judgments too early or handing over interpretive labor flattens personal narratives.
- Echo chambers: Excessive alignment produces agreeable mirrors instead of heterogenous, constructive second personas.
- Cognitive meal replacement: Fluent answers should not end thinking but instead prolong the formation of new questions.
- Technological optimism with no restraint: Progress cannot be a carte blanche pretext for experimentation.
