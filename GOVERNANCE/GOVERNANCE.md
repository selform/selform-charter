# Governance principles for selform-charter

## Maintainers
- The maintainers are the individuals or committee explicitly named in this document (update the list here when assignments change). Their role is to steward the charter’s structure, review major linguistic pivots, and ensure consistency between languages.

## Decision-making
- Stable decisions about terminology, new principles, or constitutional layers must reference a decision record in `en/decisions/` (mirror in `zh/decisions/`).
- For any agreement that affects governance (e.g., who can approve a chapter, what counts as a core principle), maintainers must achieve consensus or document the dissenting options before merging.

## Reviews and merges
- Every PR to a governance-sensitive path (charter, principles, decisions) requires at least two maintainers’ approval or a maintainers’ approval plus a 48-hour review window for objections.
- Maintainers may assign “observers” for specialized topics to gather broader perspective; those observers do not vote but may document concerns in decision records.

## Transparency
- Additions or removals in governance files should include a short changelog entry at the bottom of this file (date + summary).
- When a governance change is under discussion, link to the tracking Issue/Decision record in `en/decisions/` and mirror the notice in `zh/decisions/`.

## Maintenance cadence
- Periodically (e.g., quarterly), review whether the structure still supports your philosophical commitments. Any proposed restructuring itself becomes a decision record and repeats this process.
