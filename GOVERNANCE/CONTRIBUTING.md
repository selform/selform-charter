# Contributing to selform-charter

By submitting text, documentation, templates, or translations you affirm that you have the right to contribute it and agree to license your contribution under CC BY 4.0 (unless a different compatible license is explicitly noted and accepted by the maintainers).

## Participation workflow
- **Ideas & debates**: Start with an Issue or Discussion thread focused on the concept, principle, or question you want to explore. Provide background, references, and how it relates to existing entries.
- **Draft documents**: Once you have a stable articulation, open a PR that targets the relevant folder (e.g., `en/charter/`, `en/concepts/`, `en/dialogues/`).
- **Link context**: Every PR must cite the Issue/Discussion where the idea evolved, include the intended audience, and describe remaining open questions or risks.

## Document templates
Please use the markdown templates in `TEMPLATES/` (en and zh) for concepts, principles, questions, dialogue summaries, and decision records to keep structure consistent.

## Third-party content
- Provide a clear source citation and license for any excerpt, quote, or figure you include.
- Only include material whose license is compatible with CC BY 4.0; common practice is to link rather than copy large passages when unsure.
- If a submission mixes multiple licenses, isolate the third-party excerpt in its own block and explicitly state which license applies, so readers can distinguish the CC BY 4.0 body from the exception.

## Attribution and metadata
- Keep contributor names, handles, or preferred identifiers in the document history or at the top of the file.
- If you translate material, mark the translation status (e.g., `Status: draft / translated from ...`), and link to the canonical English version.

## Maintenance
- Maintainers reserve the right to adjust formatting, renaming, or folder placement for consistency while preserving the original meaning.
- For sensitive topics (ethics, ontology, etc.), patience and clear summaries of differing positions help maintain trust. If a disagreement persists, document all sides in a decision record rather than forcing premature consensus.
